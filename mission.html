Enterprise software companies have spent decades building powerful tools — and charging enterprise prices for them. Twomiah changes that. We build the same caliber of software for the people big tech has always ignored.

The Problem

Patched-together
spreadsheets and gut instinct.

A roofer in Georgia, a home care agency in Pennsylvania, and a dispensary in Michigan all share the same problem: the software built for them was either built for a bigger company and priced accordingly, or built by people who don't understand how the work actually gets done.

So they patch. A scheduling tool here, a CRM there, an invoicing app, a website builder, a marketing platform — each charging $50–500/month. Add it up and a small operator is paying enterprise prices anyway, for software that doesn't fit.

The big-CRM playbook is to charge per seat, sell add-ons, and force tradespeople to learn a vocabulary that was designed for a SaaS company's sales team. You shouldn't need a "Customer Success Manager" to print an invoice.

Twomiah is what happens when someone who's actually been in the field decides to build the tool themselves — and then keeps building until every vertical that asked for one has its own version.

What We Believe

Three rules.
Every product. Every time.

01
Built for the Vertical

A roofing CRM should know about storm tracking and insurance claims. A home care CRM should know about EVV compliance. A dispensary platform should know about METRC. Generic CRMs ask the user to bend; Twomiah products bend to the user.

02
Priced for the Operator

No per-seat gouging. No "Enterprise: Contact Sales." Flat tiers starting at $49/month, with all the features included that the competitors paywall as add-ons. The little guy isn't a tier — they're the customer.

03
Minutes, Not Months

Onboarding shouldn't take a quarter. Every Twomiah product is deployable in minutes — fully branded, your data, your domain. No "implementation specialist." No six-week kickoff. Sign up, log in, work.

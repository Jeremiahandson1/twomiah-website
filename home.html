The story
Where it starts

The
Junkyard
Bike

"That man didn't have money. He had time, ingenuity, and enough love to see what a kid needed."

My earliest memory is of police lights reflecting off a house. My mom left when I was 3. I spent a year in foster care with my sister when I was 8. My foster father went to the junkyard and built me a bike from spare parts so I could ride with my best friend.

That bike changed something in me. Not because it was a bike. Because someone gave a damn without being asked.

HOME exists because of that bike. Because of every kid in the system who got separated from their sibling. Because of every person sleeping outside who just needs a door they can call their own. Because of every family one bad month from losing everything.

Because no program, no policy, and no institution has ever consistently delivered the thing people actually need. Four words. That's the whole mission.

The Mission

Until we don't have homeless people unless it is a choice. Until children are safe in foster care because the right people are caring for them. Until the door that needs to be open at 3am is open at 3am. Until every kid grows up somewhere that communicates wonder instead of danger.

The engine

Above Ground
is the Dream.
Below Ground
is the Engine.

No cars on the surface. Ever. Inspired by Chicago's Lower Wacker Drive and Disney World's utilidor system — every vehicle, every delivery truck, every garage lives underground. The surface of HOME belongs entirely to people.

Every home has an underground garage. Park below. Walk up stairs. Step out your front door into a world with no cars in it.
All deliveries underground. The restaurant, the grocery, the 24hr kitchen — all stocked below grade. Never a truck on the paths.
Emergency vehicles surface anywhere in seconds through the underground network.
Glass panels in walking paths reveal the network below — science exhibit built into the ground you walk on.
Underground parking stays cool in Arizona heat. You never get into a 140 degree car.

Cross Section — Home at Address

Built to last

Independent of
Outside Systems.

☀️
Energy
Solar on every roof. Community battery storage. Surplus sold to the grid. All electric. The campus generates more than it uses and the data is displayed publicly for all residents to see.
💧
Water
Atmospheric water generators. Rainwater catchment. Greywater recycling. Natural creek management. Aquaponics. Deep well backup. Layered redundancy so no single failure leaves the community without water.
🌱
Food
Climate-controlled greenhouses year round. Aquaponics closed loop. Hydroponic towers. The campus feeds itself, supplies the 24hr kitchen, and sells surplus at the Commons farmers market.
🏗️
Built From the Earth
Rammed earth and adobe construction — ancient Arizona technology having a modern renaissance. Naturally cool in summer. Made from the land beneath it. Improves with age rather than deteriorates.
Beyond the campus

HOME Draws
The World In.

Beautiful enough that people want to come. And when they come they spend money that flows back into the mission. The visitor economy isn't the foundation — that's The Plant. But it adds revenue and does something equally important: it connects HOME to the wider world.

🍽️
Destination Dining
Restaurants worth driving for. A diner open late. A weekend farmers market open to the public. Food draws people in and the campus keeps them.
⚗️
Science & Maker Center
Field trips from every school in Arizona. Families on weekends. The campus systems — solar, aquaponics, underground network, rammed earth — become the curriculum. Wonder you can touch.
🗺️
The Campus Tour
A guided experience that tells the story of HOME. How it was built. What it's for. Who lives here. Ends with a meal at the diner. People who take this tour become advocates.
🏨
Boutique Inn
Architects, planners, educators, governments studying the model. Retreat groups. Families experiencing the campus for a weekend. Guests become ambassadors.
🎵
The Amphitheater
Concerts, festivals, performances open to the public. The campus becomes a cultural destination. Kids who live there see the outside world show up for something in their home.
🎬
Seasonal Events
A summer creek festival. Winter stargazing night. Harvest market. Makers fair. Traditions people plan around, year after year. HOME becomes part of Arizona's identity.

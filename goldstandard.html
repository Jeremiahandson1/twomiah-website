The Viridis Labs Scandal

In 2025, Viridis Labs — one of Michigan's largest cannabis testing facilities — was permanently shut down for faking lab results. $229 million in products were recalled. Founders were banned for life. Consumers had no way to know. The Gold Standard exists so this never happens again.

What They Don't Tell You

What Is
Remediation?

When a cannabis product fails lab testing, it doesn't always get destroyed. In many states, growers can "remediate" the product and resubmit it for testing. Here's what that means for you.

How Remediation Works

Step 1: Product Fails Testing

Lab finds pesticides, mold, heavy metals, or other contaminants above legal limits.

Step 2: Grower "Fixes" It

Common methods include re-drying, ozone treatment, irradiation (gamma rays or e-beam), distillation, or blending with clean material.

Step 3: Retested & "Passes"

The remediated product is retested. If contaminants are now below limits, it passes and can be sold.

Step 4: Sold to You

The product sits on the shelf with no visible indication it was ever remediated. You'd never know — unless you check The Gold Standard.

Why You Should Care

🔬 Remediation masks the problem, doesn't fix it

Irradiation kills mold spores but doesn't remove the mycotoxins they already produced. Ozone breaks down pesticides but can create harmful byproducts. The contaminants were there — remediation just gets the numbers below the threshold.

🧪 Terpenes and quality degrade

Heat treatment, irradiation, and ozone all destroy terpenes — the compounds responsible for flavor, aroma, and the entourage effect. A remediated product may test clean but taste flat and hit different.

⚠️ It signals poor growing practices

Clean growers pass on the first try. Remediation usually means something went wrong — improper pest management, contaminated growing media, or unsanitary processing conditions. Do you want to consume a product from that facility?

💰 It's cheaper than doing it right

Remediation costs a fraction of what it costs to grow clean from the start. Some operations treat it as a cost of doing business — grow cheap, remediate later, sell anyway. You're paying premium prices for a remediated product.

🚫 Most states don't require disclosure

In most states, there is no requirement to tell consumers a product was remediated. The label looks the same. The budtender doesn't know. Only the tracking system knows — and The Gold Standard.

Common Remediation Methods

☢️
Irradiation

Gamma rays or electron beams kill microbials. Used on food too, but destroys terpenes and may alter cannabinoid profiles.

💨
Ozone Treatment

Ozone gas oxidizes contaminants. Can create formaldehyde and other byproducts. Degrades terpenes significantly.

🔥
Distillation

Extract, purify, re-infuse. Strips the plant to its cannabinoids, removing contaminants but also everything that made it unique.

What The Gold Standard Does

We flag every remediated product with a visible warning. We show the remediation method and date when documented. We lower the score — because a product that failed and was fixed is not the same as a product that passed clean. You deserve to know. We make sure you do.
